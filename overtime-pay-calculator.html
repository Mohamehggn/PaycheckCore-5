How Overtime Pay Works in 2026

Federal law requires overtime pay at 1.5x your regular rate for hours worked over 40 in a workweek. This overtime pay calculator handles both federal weekly overtime and state daily overtime rules for California, Nevada, and Alaska.

Overtime vs Double Time

Time and a half (1.5x): Paid for hours 40-60 in a week, or hours 8-12 in a day in CA/NV/AK.

Double time (2x): Paid for hours over 12 in a day in California, or over 8 hours on the 7th consecutive day of work.

How to Calculate Your Overtime

1. Multiply regular hours × hourly rate
2. Multiply OT hours × hourly rate × 1.5
3. Multiply double time hours × hourly rate × 2
4. Add all three for gross pay
5. Subtract 22% estimated tax for net pay

Overtime Pay FAQs

How do you calculate overtime pay?

Overtime pay is 1.5 times your regular hourly rate for hours over 40 per week. If you make $20/hour and work 45 hours, you get $800 regular + $150 OT = $950 gross.

What is double time pay?

Double time is 2x your hourly rate. In California, you get double time for hours over 12 in a day or over 8 hours on your 7th consecutive workday.

Do salaried employees get overtime?

Most salaried employees earning over $684/week are exempt if they have executive, admin, or professional duties. Non-exempt salaried workers must receive overtime.

Is overtime taxed higher?

No. Overtime is taxed at your normal rate. But payroll may withhold at 22% flat rate, making your check seem smaller. You get any overpayment back at tax time.
