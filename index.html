For W-2 Employees

Understanding your paycheck is essential for effective budgeting and financial planning. W-2 employees have taxes automatically withheld from each paycheck, including federal income tax, state income tax, Social Security, and Medicare (FICA). Our paycheck calculator tools help you decode these deductions and plan your finances with confidence.

The standard Paycheck Calculator provides a comprehensive breakdown of your net pay after all withholdings. If you work overtime, the Overtime Pay Calculator shows exactly how much extra you earn at time-and-a-half rates. Received a bonus? The Bonus Tax Calculator uses the IRS 22% flat rate method to estimate withholdings on supplemental wages.

  • 2026 federal tax brackets updated
  • All 50 state income tax rates included
  • FICA: 6.2% Social Security + 1.45% Medicare
  • Support for pre-tax deductions: 401k, HSA, health insurance
  • Hourly, salary, biweekly, and monthly pay periods

For Freelancers & 1099 Contractors

Self-employed individuals, freelancers, and 1099 contractors face different tax obligations than W-2 employees. Instead of automatic withholding, you must pay self-employment tax (15.3%) covering both employer and employee portions of Social Security and Medicare, plus income tax through quarterly estimated payments.

Our 1099 Tax Calculator simplifies this process by calculating your self-employment tax liability, federal income tax, and recommended quarterly payment amounts. Enter your gross income and business expenses to see your true net income after taxes. The tool also estimates your tax bracket and effective tax rate to help with year-end planning.

  • Self-employment tax: 15.3% on net earnings
  • Quarterly estimated tax payment calculator
  • Business expense deductions support
  • Track deductions to lower taxable income
  • Annual and quarterly breakdowns available

International Paycheck Tools

PaycheckCore supports international workers with specialized calculators for the UK and Canada. Tax systems vary significantly by country, with different rates, brackets, and mandatory contributions that affect take-home pay.

The UK Paycheck Calculator handles PAYE (Pay As You Earn) income tax, National Insurance contributions, and Student Loan repayments using HMRC rates for the 2025/26 tax year. For Canadian workers, our Canada Paycheck Calculator includes CPP (Canada Pension Plan), EI (Employment Insurance), federal tax, and provincial tax for all 13 provinces and territories.

  • Canada: CPP, EI, Federal + Provincial tax
  • Updated for 2025-2026 tax years
  • Support for all UK tax codes and regions
  • Canadian provincial tax rates included

Budget Planning Tools

Knowing your net pay is only the first step. Our budget tools help you make smart financial decisions based on your actual take-home income. The Can I Afford Rent Calculator uses industry-standard formulas to determine safe housing budgets based on your monthly income.

The 30% rule suggests spending no more than 30% of gross income on rent, while the 28/36 rule from mortgage lenders recommends 28% for housing and 36% for total debt. The 50/30/20 budget allocates 50% to needs, 30% to wants, and 20% to savings. Our calculator runs all three methods and compares them to your actual expenses for a complete affordability picture.

  • Multiple budget rule comparisons
  • Factors in existing debt payments
  • Location-based cost adjustments
  • Monthly cash flow analysis
  • Savings goal integration

Gross pay is your total earnings before any deductions – your salary or hourly wage multiplied by hours worked. Net pay, also called take-home pay, is what remains after subtracting taxes and deductions. For a W-2 employee earning $5,000 gross per month, deductions might include $800 federal tax, $310 FICA, $200 state tax, and $300 for health insurance, leaving $3,390 net pay. Our calculators show both amounts with detailed deduction breakdowns.
Under the Fair Labor Standards Act, non-exempt employees earn time-and-a-half (1.5x regular rate) for hours worked beyond 40 in a workweek. If your regular rate is $20/hour and you work 45 hours, you earn $800 for the first 40 hours plus $150 for 5 overtime hours at $30/hour, totaling $950 gross. Our Overtime Pay Calculator handles this math automatically and shows taxes withheld on overtime earnings, giving you the actual net amount added to your paycheck.
Bonuses are supplemental wages taxed differently than regular pay. The IRS allows employers to withhold at a flat 22% federal rate (or 37% for bonuses over $1 million), which may be higher than your normal tax bracket. Alternatively, employers can use the aggregate method, adding the bonus to your regular paycheck and calculating tax as if the total were your normal pay for that period. Our Bonus Tax Calculator shows both methods so you know what to expect. Remember, if too much is withheld, you'll receive it back as a tax refund.
