How to Use the Take Home Pay Calculator

This take home pay calculator shows your exact net paycheck after federal taxes, state taxes, FICA, 401k, and health insurance. Perfect for budgeting and comparing job offers.

Step 1: Enter Your Gross Pay

Select Annual Salary or Hourly Wage. For salary, enter your yearly amount. For hourly, enter your hourly rate and hours per week.

Step 2: Choose Pay Frequency

Select how often you get paid: Weekly (52 paychecks), Biweekly (26), Semi-Monthly (24), or Monthly (12). Most W-2 employees are paid biweekly.

Step 3: Set Filing Status & State

Your filing status and state determine tax rates. Single filers pay more than married. States like CA, NY have higher rates than TX, FL (no state tax).

Step 4: Add Deductions

Include 401k % and monthly health insurance cost. The calculator applies standard FICA (7.65%) and estimates federal tax based on 2026 brackets.

What is Take Home Pay?

  • Take Home Pay: Your net paycheck - the actual money deposited in your bank account
  • Gross Pay: Total earnings before any deductions or taxes
  • Deductions: Federal tax, state tax, FICA (Social Security + Medicare), 401k, health insurance
  • Average: Most people take home 65-75% of gross pay after all deductions

Common Take Home Pay Examples 2026

  • $50,000 salary: ~$3,100-$3,400/month take home (biweekly: $1,550-$1,700)
  • $75,000 salary: ~$4,500-$4,900/month take home (biweekly: $2,250-$2,450)
  • $100,000 salary: ~$5,800-$6,300/month take home (biweekly: $2,900-$3,150)
  • $120,000 salary: ~$6,800-$7,400/month take home (biweekly: $3,400-$3,700)
  • $25/hour (40 hrs): ~$3,250/month take home ($52k salary equivalent)
  • $35/hour (40 hrs): ~$4,500/month take home ($72.8k salary equivalent)

Why Calculate Take Home Pay?

  • Budget Accurately: Plan rent, mortgage, and bills using actual income, not gross
  • Job Offers: Compare take home pay between salary vs hourly positions
  • Tax Planning: See how 401k contributions reduce taxable income and increase take home %
  • Loan Applications: Lenders use gross income, but you budget with net income
  • Salary Negotiation: Know your target take home pay to negotiate gross salary

Pro Tip: To maximize take home pay, increase 401k/HSA contributions (pre-tax), claim correct W-4 allowances, or move to a no-income-tax state like Texas, Florida, or Washington.
