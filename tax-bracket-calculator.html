How to Use the Tax Bracket Calculator

Find your 2026 federal income tax bracket and calculate how much federal tax you owe. This calculator uses official IRS tax brackets for 2026.

Step 1: Enter Taxable Income

Input your annual taxable income. This is your gross income minus standard deduction ($14,600 single, $29,200 married) or itemized deductions.

Step 2: Select Filing Status

Choose Single, Married Filing Jointly, Married Filing Separately, or Head of Household. Your filing status determines your tax bracket thresholds.

Understanding Tax Brackets vs Effective Rate

  • Marginal Tax Rate: The rate you pay on your last dollar of income. If you're in the 22% bracket, only income above $47,150 (single) is taxed at 22%.
  • Effective Tax Rate: Your actual overall tax rate. Total tax divided by total income. Always lower than marginal rate.
  • Progressive System: You don't pay your bracket rate on all income. Income is taxed in chunks at 10%, 12%, 22%, etc.
